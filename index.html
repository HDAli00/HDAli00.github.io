Capgemini · ASML · Platform infrastructure at scale

Engineering is just puzzle-solving with stakes. That's what drew me in — at Fugro it was levee calculations and hydraulic models, figuring out how water behaves under pressure. Alongside that I got into data science, a new arena to explore with the same mindset.

From there the curiosity kept going. Data science led naturally into data engineering — PySpark pipelines, Azure Synapse, Databricks — and then into platform work, where the question shifted from "does the pipeline run" to "can anyone actually see what it's doing." That's what brought me to observability. At ASML that meant building the instrumentation layer — OpenTelemetry, Grafana, Kubernetes — the infrastructure other engineers rely on without thinking about it.

The exploration continues — platform engineering, cloud architecture, and everything that connects data to production systems at scale. This is where I'm going deep next. Follow along.
